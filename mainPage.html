Investing made easy

Stonks

Buying shares of a company makes you a shareholder, allowing you to participate in the company's success and potentially earn a share of its profits. Stocks can offer the potential for high returns but also come with higher risk due to market fluctuations.

Bonds

Bonds are essentially loans to governments, municipalities, or corporations. When you buy a bond, you're lending money in exchange for periodic interest payments and the return of the bond's face value at maturity. Bonds are generally considered l ower risk compared to stocks.

Cryptocurrency

Digital currencies like Bitcoin and Ethereum have gained popularity as alternative investments. They can be highly volatile and speculative, so careful research is essential.

401k or Roth IRA

These retirement accounts offer tax advantages for long-term savings. They often provide a range of investment options, including stocks, bonds, and mutual funds.
